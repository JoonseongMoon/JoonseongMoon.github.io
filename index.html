About

I'm a graduate student at Carnegie Mellon University studying at the intersection of building science, sustainable design, and artificial intelligence.

My work spans building energy modeling using tools like IES VE and Revit, sustainable design strategies informed by environmental analysis, and digital twin research focused on data quality and uncertainty quantification.

I believe the built environment is ripe for intelligent transformation. By combining physics-based simulation with computational design and modern ML techniques, we can create buildings and spaces that are more energy-efficient, resilient, and responsive to both occupants and climate.
