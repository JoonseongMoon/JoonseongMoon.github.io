Parametric Design

Environmental Site Analysis & Parametric Park Design

A three-part project for Mong Kwok Ping Garden (HKU) combining Grasshopper-driven environmental site analysis, data-driven surface manipulation, and detailed parametric construction design.

Year 2024
Role Environmental Site Analysis Coursework
Tools Grasshopper · Rhino · Ladybug
Site Mong Kwok Ping Garden, HKU
Part 1

Site Analysis

The first phase focuses on understanding the environmental conditions of Mong Kwok Ping Garden at HKU. Using Grasshopper and Ladybug, the site was modeled in 3D to capture its topography, followed by a series of analyses: slope analysis to map terrain gradients, sun path analysis to trace solar trajectories throughout the year, and direct sunlight hours analysis to quantify solar exposure across the site surface. These analyses form the data foundation for all subsequent design decisions.

Part 2

Data-Driven Design Development

The second phase translates the site analysis data into design. This part focuses on specific user identification—understanding who will use the garden and how—and develops a data-driven surface manipulation process that responds to the environmental findings from Part 1. The approach is site-specific: terrain deformations, shade structures, and programmatic zones are generated based on sunlight exposure, wind comfort thresholds, and user activity patterns rather than arbitrary formal decisions.

Part 2 report — user identification, data-driven surface manipulation, and site-specific design approach
Part 3

Construction & Detailed Design

The final phase takes the parametric design from Part 2 into buildable detail. This includes dimensioning the overall structure, defining material specifications, detailing connection systems, and producing construction-ready drawings. The design maintains the environmental responsiveness established in earlier phases while meeting practical requirements for fabrication and assembly.
